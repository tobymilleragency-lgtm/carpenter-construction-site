Scope checklist

Core decisions for a clean basements & bonus rooms estimate

Existing condition

Before pricing the work, the contractor needs to understand what is currently in place. Photos should show the full room or exterior area, close-up problem spots, access points, utilities nearby, and any previous repairs. For basement and bonus room finishing, the existing condition often determines whether the job is straightforward or needs more careful sequencing.

Desired finish

Homeowners should be ready to explain the finished outcome, not just the complaint. That includes the look, use, durability expectations, and any must-have details. Finish decisions affect materials, order of operations, and whether specialty trade partners need to be scheduled under the GC plan.

Access and timing

Every project affects daily life. A good plan asks where crews can park, how materials enter, what areas need dust or weather protection, whether pets or tenants are involved, and what time-sensitive events are already on the calendar. Those details shape the build plan as much as the visible work.
